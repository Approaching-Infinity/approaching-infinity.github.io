Nobody sees the first two years. A fintech founder who has built financial infrastructure from scratch joins us to talk about the earliest phase of building a fund or financial company: the legal plumbing, the operational stack, and why credibility compounds slower, and matters more, than anything else.

## In this episode

- The unglamorous checklist: entities, service providers, and operational plumbing
- Why compliance and transparency are assets, not overhead, for a new manager
- Earning trust before assets: reputation as the first form of capital
- Choosing your stack: what to build, what to buy, what to postpone
- The emotional reality of the zero-to-one phase, and what keeps founders going
