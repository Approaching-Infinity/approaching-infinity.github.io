Everyone has a risk framework until the market tests it. This week a risk manager walks us through what real risk management looks like: limits set in advance, sizing that respects uncertainty, and the psychology of following your own rules when it hurts.

## In this episode

- Why survival, not optimization, is the first job of risk management
- Setting hard limits before the trade, and what makes people break them
- Position sizing as the most underrated decision in any portfolio
- Leverage, liquidity, and the difference between volatility and ruin
- Post-mortems: turning a drawdown into a durable process improvement
