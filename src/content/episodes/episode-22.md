The systematic versus discretionary debate is usually framed as a fight. Our guest argues it is really a division of labor. We dig into which decisions belong to the model, which belong to the human, and how the best processes make that boundary explicit.

## In this episode

- What "systematic" actually means day to day, beyond the marketing
- The decisions that should never be left to in-the-moment judgment
- Overrides: when a human should step in, and how to log it honestly
- Research discipline: avoiding the backtest that only works on paper
- How small teams can borrow systematic habits without a quant stack
